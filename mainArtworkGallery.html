Out of the Office

I spend far too much time sitting infront of a computer screen. Its the new norm for most of us. Now, I'm not complaining, as designing, developing and creating is a deep passion of mine. Still, everything in moderation. After a long day of basking in the gentle glow of florescent lighting, it can be good to get a little natural light. When I'm not working, I'm in the workshop cooking up the next big project.

Artist Statement

"Iâ€™m drawn to clay not for its inherent beauty or form, but for its potential. In its ambiguity I find the joy of working with clay, for it has no preconceptions of what it should be. I explore these ideas through repetition and intricacy, and I am always looking for ways to embellish simple forms and shapes. A large emphasis is placed on strong lines, sometimes the overall shape following the guides of a simple rectangle. The result is a piece that is often asymmetrical and unbalanced.

The use of machinery is kept to a minimum, and instead I will often trace, cut, and place pieces individually, the mark of the hand always being present.The pieces are often short of being exact or perfect. Every square, tile and chunk handled is placed methodically, creating an overwhelming sense of time and effort. The work acts as a visual curiosity, drawing viewers in to not only see the piece, but also to experience it."
